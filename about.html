A bit about me

I've always been curious about how things work, which is what pulled me toward computer science in the first place, that and wanting to build things that actually make a difference for people. Outside of coursework, I gravitate toward graphics programming in my free time; there's something satisfying about building a world from scratch, pixel by pixel, light by light.

Tutoring has been a constant since I was 14, back when I started answering questions on Brainly. It's turned into one of the most rewarding parts of what I do: there's nothing quite like watching someone finally get something they'd been stuck on. That's carried through UCSC, through Upwork, through every student I've worked with since.

When I'm not coding or tutoring, I'm usually volunteering at a local food bank or shelter, at church, or just enjoying the beach. (Also, I've always had a soft spot for . And yes, pink is my favorite color, in case the site didn't give it away.)
